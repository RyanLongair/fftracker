Key Items

Kvanilla - No key item randomization Kmain - Randomized Key Items - The locations of key items are shuffled amongst each other. Ksummon - Mix with summon quest rewards - Key items may be awarded by the Sylphs, the king and queen of the Feymarch, Odin's throne, and Cave Bahamut. Kmoon - Mix with moon boss rewards - Key items may be awarded by the bosses of the Lunar Subterrane. Ktrap - Mix with trapped chests - Key items may be awarded by trapped chest rewards. Kunsafe - No safety checks - Normally, you are guaranteed a path to the underworld that does not require first going to the moon. This flag removes that check.

Characters

Cvanilla - No character randomization Cstandard - Randomize characters - Characters join your party in the same locations as the original game, but the character you meet there is randomized. Crelaxed - Randomize characters (relaxed) - Unlike the standard randomization, characters are equally likely to appear in any position. Cmaybe - Characters not guaranteed to appear - Normally, the randomizer will try to include every eligible character in the game at least once. This flag removes that logic, meaning some eligible characters may not appear. Cdistinct - X distinct characters Excluded Characters - Specific Character - Starting Character - Cj:spells - Characters learn J-spells - White mages will learn Armor, Shell, and Dispel. All mages learn spells at the levels they would in FF4j. Cj:abilities - Characters have J-commands - Characters retain the battle commands from FF4j that were removed in FF4us. Cnodupes - Duplicate characters not allowed - If a duplicate character tries to join your party, instead nothing happens. Cbye - Dismissed characters are irretrievable - Dismissed characters cannot be retrieved. Cpermajoin - Characters can't be dismissed - You have the choice of whether to accept each new party member, but once your party is full, you cannot recruit anyone else. Cpermadeath - Permadeath - Party members that are "swoon" at the end of any battle are permanently removed from the game. Cpermadeader - Permadeader - Party members that are swoon or stone at the end of any battle are permanently removed from the game.

Treasures

Tvanilla - No chest randomization Tshuffle - Shuffle chests - Treasure contents are the same as in original FF4, but their positions are shuffled. This randomization is weighted so that overworld treasures tend to remain in the overworld, and similarly for underworld/moon treasures. Tstandard - Standard chest randomization - A basic randomization, allowing items of reasonable strength to appear. Items are equally likely to appear in any chest regardless of location. Tpro - Pro chest randomization - A skill-testing randomization, limiting the strength of available items, and weighted to favor stronger items in later-game and infrequently-visited areas. Twild - Wild chest randomization - An unrestricted randomization, allowing equal chance for items to appear in any chest, including the most powerful items. Twildish - Wild-ish chest randomization - Allows items of any strength to appear, including the most powerful items, but uses location-based weighting like the pro randomization. Tempty - Chests are empty Tsparse - Sparse Options - Only XX% of chests are filled. Tno:j - No J-Items in chests Tjunk - Keep junk - Normally, poor-quality items in chests are replaced with an equivalent amount of GP to reduce inventory bloat. Enable this flag if you want to keep them as items.

Shops

Svanilla - No shop randomization Sshuffle - Shuffle shops - The same items are available for sale in shops as in original FF4, but their locations are shuffled. This randomization is weighted so that overworld shop items tend to remain on the overworld, and similarly for underworld/moon shops. Sstandard - Standard shop randomization - A basic randomization, allowing items of moderate strength to appear in shops, and weighted to place stronger items in later-game shops. Spro - Pro shop randomization - A skill-testing randomization, limiting the strength of available items, and weighted to place stronger items in shops gated behind key items. Swild - Wild shop randomization - An unrestricted randomization, allowing equal chance for items to appear in any shop, including the most powerful items. Scabins - Shops sell only cabins Sempty - Shops sell nothing Sunsafe - No safety checks - Normally, the randomizer guarantees Cure2 potions, Life potions and StarVeils are readily available, and that Cure3 potions are available in seeds without white mages. This flag removes those checks. Sfree - Shop items are free Squarter - Items sell for 1/4 price Sno:j - No J-items in shops Sno:apples - No Apples/Soma Drops in shops Sno:sirens - No Sirens in shops

Bosses

Bvanilla - No boss randomization Bstandard - Randomize bosses - The positions of bosses are shuffled. Boss stats are roughly scaled to match the battle it's replacing. Bunsafe - No safety checks - Normally, you are guaranteed a path to the underworld that does not require defeating the most difficult bosses. This flag removes that check. Balt:gauntlet - Alt Gauntlet - Replaces the Fabul Gauntlet boss with five unscaled normal enemy encounters from the nearby area. Bwhyburn - Disable Wyvern's opening MegaNuke Bwhichburn - Replace Wyvern's opening MegaNuke with random attack - Usually, what you get is better than a MegaNuke. Usually.

Challenges

Nchars - No Free Characters - Characters will not join your party in the Watery Pass, Damcyan, Mysidia, or Mt. Ordeals. Nkey - No Free Key Item - Edward in Toroia will not give you a key item. Instead, a key item may be earned from Rydia's mom in Mist, who will appear after you have found and defeated the Mist Dragon. Nbosses - No Free Bosses - All boss battle enemies have the "boss bit" set. The alternate win conditions are removed from the D.Knight, Karate, K/Q.Eblan, and WaterHag fights.

Encounters

Evanilla - Original encounter rate Etoggle - Random encounters can be toggled - Encounters can be enabled/disabled from the in-game Custom menu. Ereduce - Random encounters reduced by 50% Enoencounters - No random encounters Ekeep:doors - Preserve TrapDoor fights - Prevents the above encounter rate setting from applying to the TrapDoor fights in the Sealed Cave. Ekeep:behemoths - Preserve forced Behemoth fights - Prevents the above encounter rate setting from applying to the forced Behemoth fights in Cave Bahamut. Edanger - Keep dangerous encounters - Back attacks and "Surprised!" encounters will still occur, even when encounters are disabled via Etoggle or Enoencounters. Eno:sirens - Remove Sirens from drop/sneak tables Eno:jdrops - Use US version drop/sneak tables Ecantrun - Can't run from battles Enoexp - No EXP for random encounters - Bosses and trapped chest fights still award EXP.

Other

-kit:minimal - Minimal starter kit - You begin the game with just a few basic utility potions and items. -kit:basic - Basic starter kit - You begin the game with an assortment of basic utility potions and items. -kit:better - Better starter kit - You begin the game with an assortment of basic utility potions and items, and a few extra special-use combat items for sticky situations. -kit:loaded - Loaded starter kit - You begin the game with a wide array of utility potions and items. -kit:spitball - "Spitball" starter kit - You begin the game with a randomized subset of the item types found in the "loaded" starter kit (though the quantities you receive of each may exceed what you would get in the loaded kit). -noadamants - No Adamant Armor - Adamant Armors are not available. Trading the Pink Tail in the Adamant Grotto instead grants a strong item. -vintage - Vintage Battlefield - Enables the April Fools Day 2019 mode. -spoon - Edward can equip the Spoon -vanilla:fusoya - Vintage FuSoYa - By default, Free Enterprise runs with the "FuSoYa Challenge", where FuSoYa begins with 500 HP and a collection of low-level spells, and each boss defeated grants him 100 HP and three random additional spells. This flag disables that challenge and restores vanilla's FuSoYa behavior, where he joins with 1900 HP and his full spellset. -vanilla:agility - Vanilla agility anchoring - FF4 scales the speed of combat based on the agility stat of your party's "agility anchor". In Free Enterprise, the anchor is always the first occupied party slot (in the sequence of middle, top, bottom, top-middle, bottom-middle). However, in vanilla FF4, if Cecil is in the party, then he is the agility anchor, regardless of position. Enabling this flag will restore that behavior, making the first Cecil in your party the anchor, if present. -vanilla:hobs - Vanilla Hobs spell - By default, instead of learning Fire1 at Mt. Hobs, Rydia will learn a random summon (other than Leviatan and Bahamut). This flag removes that behavior. -vanilla:exp - Vanilla EXP distribution - To reduce grinding, Free Enterprise normally applies a number of experience boosts: (1) EXP is not divided among the party, each member receives the full amount. (2) Once 10 key items have been collected, earned EXP is doubled. (3) In a full party, low-level party members (at least 5 levels below the median level) earn double EXP. Enabling this flag removes all of these boosts, and distributes EXP as in original FF4. -vanilla:fashion - Vanilla fashion - To help distinguish duplicate characters, Free Enterprise randomizes the battle palettes of each character. Enabling this flag preserves each character's original battle colors. -vanilla:traps - Vanilla trapped chest locations - By default, trapped chests (ie. those with Monsters!) are relocated randomly within the same dungeon/area. This flag forces trapped chests to remain in their original positions. -vanilla:z - Vanilla Z Sprite - Free Enterprise replaces Zeromus' battle sprite with a random sprite. (Its battle behavior and stats are unchanged.) Enable this flag to preserve Zeromus' original battle sprite.
